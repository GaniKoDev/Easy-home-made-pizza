Easy Homemade Italian Pizza

Personally, my best evenings with friends or family are usually around pizzas! I love making it, with the dough (sometimes thin and crispy, I also like the thicker “bread” style pizza dough).

Here is my personal recipe (I know there are hundreds), and also my favorite garnish: tomato sauce with homemade herbs,

mozzarella, fresh tomatoes, Italian raw ham and fresh arugula (basil would have been so hot) .

The dough is very quick to make (especially if you use a bread machine or a food processor) and should rise for 1 hour. Everything else is child's play,

the garnish, I make it with my guests who choose what they want (tomato base or fresh cream, chorizo, smoked turkey, Parma ham or anchovies and olives...the one my mother favorite ).

Bonna Petit

by :Gani

How many persons : 4 people Preparation time : 30 mins Cooking time : 10 minutes

List of ingredients

For the dough (3 pizzas with these proportions)

  • 500 g flour (or a mixture of 250 g normal flour + 250 g bread flour)
  • 1 C. salt
  • 1 C. olive oil
  • 1 sachet of dehydrated baker's yeast (about 6 to 8 g in general)
  • 250ml water at room temperature

For garnish :

  • 2 Onions
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 300 ml passata or tomato coulis
  • 1 tbsp. coffee Provencal herbs (thyme, oregano, rosemary, etc.)
  • 2 or 3 fresh tomatoes or cherry tomatoes (optional)
  • Mozzarella or Emmental type grated cheese
  • Black olives
  • Italian ham chiffonade
  • Rocket

Recipe steps:

  1. In a bowl, make a well with the flour, and put the salt on one side, the yeast on the other
    (make sure not to put the salt and the yeast in contact at the start)
  2. Add the water in the center, mix to obtain a homogeneous dough, and knead for 10 minutes by
    hand on the work surface or using a bread machine or a robot with the dough hook
  3. Add the olive oil and knead for another 5 minutes
  4. Cover with cling film in contact with the dough, and let rise for 1 hour at room temperature,
    the dough should double in volume
  5. Remove the air from the dough, pressing it with your hand, then divide
    the dough into 2 or 3 balls depending on the number of pizzas desired
  6. Let the balls of dough rest for 30 minutes, covering them with cling film
  7. Roll out each ball of dough on the work surface (if the dough shrinks,
    avoid flouring your work surface, it will stick and spread more easily).
  8. Arrange the circle of dough on a plate with a sheet of baking paper
    and garnish it with the pizza sauce before baking

Preparing the pizza sauce

  1. Fry the minced onion and garlic in a little olive oil, add the tomato coulis, the Provence herbs
    (thyme, rosemary, or oregano) and simmer for 10 minutes over low heat.
  2. Spread a little tomato sauce on the pizza dough
  3. Distribute the filling evenly
    (cut tomatoes, mozzarella, ham, etc...) >> it's up to you to give free rein to your imagination :)
  4. Bake in the bottom of the oven for 10 minutes at 220°C, very hot oven
  5. Serve with arugula... it stings a little, it's excellent :)
